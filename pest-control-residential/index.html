Common Problems Addressed by Residential Pest Control

Unexplained Bites or Welts

Finding unexplained bites or welts on your skin, especially after waking up, could be a sign of bed bugs, fleas, or other biting insects. These pests can cause significant discomfort and potential health issues, making professional pest control essential to identify and eradicate the source of the bites.

Strange Noises in Walls or Ceilings

Hearing scratching, scurrying, or gnawing sounds in your walls or ceilings often indicates a rodent infestation. Rodents can cause structural damage, contaminate food, and spread diseases. Addressing these noises early can prevent further damage and health risks.

Visible Pests

Seeing ants, roaches, spiders, or other pests in your home is a clear sign of an infestation. Pests can contaminate food, spread diseases, and create an unsanitary environment. A pest control service can identify the source of the infestation and implement effective treatment solutions.

Damaged Wood or Structures

Finding damaged wood or structures around your property can indicate a termite or carpenter ant infestation. These pests can cause severe structural damage over time, leading to costly repairs. Early detection and treatment are crucial to protect your home.

Unusual Pet Behavior

If your pets are scratching excessively, biting at their fur, or displaying other unusual behaviors, they may be infested with fleas or ticks. These pests can cause skin irritation, allergies, and transmit diseases to your pets and potentially to you.

What is Integrated Pest Management (IPM)?

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) is a comprehensive approach to pest control that focuses on long-term prevention and control through a combination of techniques. This includes biological control, habitat manipulation, modification of cultural practices, and the use of resistant varieties. Pesticides are used only when necessary and are selected and applied in a manner that minimizes risks to human health, non-target organisms, and the environment.

How Can I Tell if I Have Termites?

Signs of termite infestation include mud tubes on exterior walls, discarded wings near windows or doors, and hollow-sounding wood. If you suspect a termite infestation, it's crucial to schedule a professional inspection to assess the extent of the damage and implement effective treatment solutions.
